Manti

Kyrgyz manti are hearty, steamed Central Asian dumplings featuring a spiced filling of ground lamb or beef, onions, and sometimes diced potatoes or pumpkin, encased in a thin dough, then served hot with yogurt, butter, or sour cream for a comforting, flavorful meal.

Kyrgyz manti, like other Central Asian varieties, originated with ancient Turkic and Mongol nomadic peoples as a practical, portable food.

Make the Dough

In a bowl, mix flour and salt Slowly add warm water and knead until you get a firm, smooth dough Cover the dough and let it rest for 20–30 minutes.

Prepare the filling

In a bowl, mix flour and salt,Slowly add warm water and knead until you get a firm, smooth dough,Cover the dough and let it rest for 20–30 minutes.

Roll and shape the manti

Divide the dough into several small balls Roll each ball into a thin sheet (about 1–2 mm) Cut into squares about 4–5 inches (10–12 cm) Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each square Fold the dough and pinch the edges together to form the traditional manti shape.

Steam the Manti

Grease the steamer trays so the manti don’t stick Place manti with a little space between them Steam for 40–45 minutes until fully cooked.
